(This is an simple data visulization analysis of the Ted dataset from Ted 1984 to Ted 2017)




1. what jobs do Ted speakers usually do?


The bar chart shows all the occupations that more than 10 speakers engaged in. We can see that among all 971 speakers, there are 18 people work as inventors which is the top 1 job that speakers engaged in. And 17 writers, 16 designers, 14 artists, and 11 architects, 11 filmmakers, 11 psychologists.




2.Is there any correlation between views and the lengths of its title?


This scatter plot chart shows the relation between the length of title and views of these 971 teds. The x axis refers to how many words consist the title and y axis refers to the number of views.

From this chart, we can see that most ted talk's title are of 4 to 7 letters. It's hard to say if the speaker use title of 4 to 7 words will gain high views, but when the talk has a too long title like over 12 words, the views are apprantly relatively low.




3.What topics speakers prefer talking about?

Among these 971 ted talks, 38 of them talk about culture, and 35 talk about technology, and 24 are about entertainment. These are top 3 topics that speakers always talk about.




4. Is there any correlation between views,
duration, comments, and the number of languages translated to?

In this bubble chart, x axis refers to the duration and y axis refers to the number of views; the radius of bubbles means the number of comments; and the opicity means how many languages this ted video has been traslated to, if it's translated to more language, it will be more opaque.

And when you put the mouse cursor on the certain circle, its name will show up at the bottom of the chart.

From the chart we can see that people prefer the duration of 17-22 minutes , and if the talk is too long, people will not click.

And when it comes to the comments, except for one outlier "militant athrism", it's positive correlation between the number of views and comments. And when the duration is among 17-22 minutes, the comments will be relatively high, too.
